Math for AI Seminars

The Math for AI seminars are primarily given by University of Alberta students studying Artificial Intelligence. See the schedule below for dates, times, topics, and links.

Our goal is to collaboratively build the community's mathematical toolbox. We have a diverse academic background, so it's likely that everyone knows something which another could use. This seminar series is a platform for us to share those mathematical tools.

Anyone is welcome to attend without registration. If you're interested in giving a talk, please contact us. If an event in the calendar below does not yet have a description, then it is open for a speaker to take! Note the presenter guidelines below.

Schedule

The calendar events are updated as we book speakers. If an event does not have a speaker or topic listed, then we are looking for a topic lead for that day. (That could be you! Just reach out.)

To stay up to date, add the calendar to your own with the "+" button in the calendar's bottom right!

Guidelines for Presenters

If you're interested in presenting, here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Each talk should cover a mathematical topic that is useful (in your view) to AI researchers.
  • You are not obligated to connect the mathematics to AI research, but you are encouraged to do so if the connection is not obvious.
  • It's helpful if you recommend resources further exploring your topic.
  • Slides or whiteboard are most common, but any format is allowed.
  • Your talk should last about 30 minutes, leaving time for questions and discussion.
  • If desired, a follow up survey will be sent to the audience, and the results will be made available to you.
  • Talks are not recorded for publication or future viewing unless explicitly noted. If a talk is recorded, it will be noted in the calendar event and before the talk starts.
