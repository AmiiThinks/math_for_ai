Math for AI Seminars

The Math for AI seminars are primarily given by University of Alberta students studying Artificial Intelligence. The talks are held biweekly on Wednesdays in CSC 3-33. See the schedule below for specific dates, times, and topics.

Our goal is to collaboratively build our community's mathematical toolbox. We have a diverse academic background, so it's likely that everyone has a mathematical tool that someone else could use. This seminar series is a platform for our resident experts to share those tools. Hence, the seminar topics should focus on mathematics.

Anyone is welcome to attend without registration. If you're interested in giving a talk, then email Yash or Kirby. If an event in the calendar below does not yet have a description, then it is open for a speaker to take. Note the presenter guidelines below!

Guidelines for Presenters

If you're interested in presenting, here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Each talk should cover a mathematical topic that is useful (in your view) to AI researchers.
  • You are not obligated to connect the ideas to AI, but you are encouraged to do so if the connection is not obvious.
  • It's helpful if you recommend resources further exploring your topic.
  • You may use slides, whiteboard, or both.
  • A short talk description needs to be submitted to Yash or Kirby, ideally at least a week in advance..
  • An organizer introduce you if desired.
  • Your talk should start soon after 2:00 pm, and last about 30 minutes, leaving time for questions and discussion.
  • It's your responsibility to pass over any disruptive questions if they divert from the talk.
  • If desired, a follow up survey will be sent to the audience, and the results will be made available to you.
  • Talks are not recorded for publication or future viewing.
